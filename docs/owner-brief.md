# Owner Brief: Route Mates

## Project Vision
Build the most practical and trusted Hyderabad-first route-matching platform for daily city travel, focused on recurring utility over hype.

## Problem Statement
Many existing commute/carpool products feel inconsistent for local daily use: low route density in specific corridors, inactive listings, weak trust signals, and high coordination friction. Hyderabad commuters need a simpler way to find people already traveling similar routes.

## Core Idea
Users post recurring or one-time routes with source, destination, and time window. The platform matches users on route and schedule overlap. Users then coordinate directly and may share costs themselves.

## What the Product Is
- A route-matching platform
- A trust-first local mobility network
- A recurring city commute coordination product

## What the Product Is Not
- Not a taxi app
- Not a ride-hailing operator
- Not a fleet business
- Not a driver marketplace

## Initial Market & Hyderabad Focus
Start in Hyderabad with corridor-first density strategy:
- Kondapur / Miyapur / Kukatpally → HITEC City
- Gachibowli / Financial District routes
- Apartment-to-metro and college commute patterns

## Target User Segments
- Daily office commuters
- College students
- Apartment and gated community residents
- Metro first/last-mile travelers
- Women seeking trusted local route groups

## Product Principles
- Simplicity first
- Trust before scale
- Local route density before city expansion
- Recurring usefulness over one-off novelty
- Clear user control and low friction coordination

## MVP Scope
Must-have:
- User onboarding and profile basics
- Route posting (recurring and one-time)
- Route/time overlap matching
- Match notifications
- Direct coordination channel (in-app or handoff)
- Basic trust markers and reporting controls

Out of scope for MVP:
- Platform-managed transport operations
- Taxi-style dispatch workflows
- Complex pricing engines

## Monetization Phases
1. **Phase 1 (Free):** Build route density, trust, and retention.
2. **Phase 2 (Premium):** Add paid power-user features (priority visibility, advanced filters, recurring automation).
3. **Phase 3 (B2B/Communities):** Paid private networks for apartments, colleges, and organizations.

## Key Risks
- Low initial route density
- Safety/trust concerns
- Fake or low-quality listings
- Retention drop if coordination friction remains high
- Product drift toward taxi-style marketplace behavior

## Strategic Positioning
Position Route Mates as:
- “Find people already going your way.”
- “Hyderabad-first trusted route matching.”

Never position Route Mates as:
- “Cheap taxi alternative”
- “Ride-hailing marketplace”
